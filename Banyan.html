Banyan

Scientific Name: Ficus benghalensis
Native to:

  • Ficus microcarpa is native to India, Pakistan, Nepal, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, China, Taiwan, the Malay Archipelago,Mainland Southeast Asia, New Guinea, Australia

  • The Central American Banyan (Ficus pertusa) is native to Central America and northern South America.

  • The shortleaf fig (Ficus citrifolia) is native to South Florida, th Carribean islands, Central America, and South America south to Paraguay.

  • The Florida strangler fig (Ficus aurea) is also native to South Florida and the Carribean islands.

  • The Moreton Bay fig (Ficus macrophylla) and Port Jackson fig (Ficus rubiginosa) are other related species.


Scientific Classification

Kingdom: Plantae
Order: Rosales
Family: Moraceae
Genus: Ficus
Subgenus: F. Urostigma

Characterisitics

Banyans bear their fruit in the form of a structure called a "syconium". The syconium of Ficus species supply shelter and food for fig wasps and the trees depend on the fig wasps for pollination.

The leaves of the banyan tree are large, leathery, glossy, green, and elliptical. Like most figs, the leaf bud is covered by two large scales. As the leaf develops the scales abscise. Young leaves have an attractive reddish tinge.

Older banyan trees are characterized by aerial prop roots that mature into thick, woody trunks, which can become indistinguishable from the primary trunk with age.
